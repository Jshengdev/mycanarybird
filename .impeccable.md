## Design Context

### Users
VCs evaluating the product for investment, and potential customers who are primarily developers building computer-use AI agents. They're assessing whether Canary is credible, well-built, and solves a real problem. The landing page is both a pitch deck and a product introduction.

### Brand Personality
Sharp, fast, smooth. Canary is a precision instrument — it watches everything, catches mistakes instantly, and gets out of your way. Not loud or flashy, but unmistakably competent.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode
- **Visual tone**: Clean and spacious like braintrust.dev (generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, confident typography). Textured and tactile like uselemma.ai (ASCII art patterns, monospace accents, technical personality).
- **Signature element**: The ASCII dither/block hover effect (AsciiHover) — this is the one thing people should remember. SVG blocks that unfurl on hover, cursor-reactive glyph backgrounds.
- **References**: braintrust.dev (interaction, colors, spacing, clarity), uselemma.ai (texture, formatting)
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS templates, gradient text, glowing dark-mode dashboards, rounded-everything with drop shadows

### Design Principles
1. **Precision over decoration** — Every element should feel measured and intentional. No filler, no padding for padding's sake.
2. **Typography carries the hierarchy** — Let type scale and weight do the work, not color or ornament.
3. **Texture through restraint** — The ASCII patterns and monospace accents provide all the visual interest needed. Don't add more.
4. **Confidence reads as trust** — Generous whitespace, unhurried pacing, and clean structure signal that the product is solid.
5. **The hover is the brand** — AsciiHover is the signature interaction. It should feel fast, smooth, and satisfying on every interactive element.
